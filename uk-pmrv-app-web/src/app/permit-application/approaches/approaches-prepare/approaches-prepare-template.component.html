Preparing to define monitoring approaches

The monitoring approach is the part of a monitoring plan that defines how annual emissions are monitored.

In general, the method and degree of accuracy required in monitoring emissions becomes greater as the amount of emissions increases - and is based on two factors:

  • The overall estimated annual emissions - as defined in the installation category task
  • The individual estimated emissions of a fuel or material source stream

Prior to starting to define monitoring approaches, you should consider and prepare the following for each of your fuel and material source streams:

  • an estimate of how many tonnes of CO2e are emitted annually
  • the measurement devices or methods you will use to monitor and record emissions

You may also need to consider that your installation may release emissions from a fuel or material source stream in different amounts within different parts of your installation and that these may be monitored by a different set of measurement devices or methods.
